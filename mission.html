The goal of the Undergraduate Surgery Interest Group is to create unique opportunities for pre-medical students to explore surgery and gain exposure to the thought processes of clinical decision-making. We recognize that our members are at a relatively early step in their higher education, and this is an important time to to discover and explore the reasons why we want to become physicians. As such, our ultimate goal is to immerse students in settings where they can appreciate the complexity, significance, and beauty of the work that characterizes not only surgery, but all of medicine.

The following are the educational concepts we work to bring to our members:
  • The thought processes surrounding the decision-making of surgery
  • How surgery can be used to treat disease
  • How surgery is integrated into other components of clinical medicine
  • Ethical challenges faced by physicians and surgeons
  • Significant paradigms and advances that characterize the history of medicine
  • How surgery can be viewed as a form of art
  • Preparing for a changing surgical field increasingly enriched with advances in robotics and biomedical engineering
  • The revolution in gene-targeted therapies and surgery’s future role in combination therapies
  • Socioeconomic disparities in healthcare access
  • Surgical training and practice in refugee settings
  • Integrating surgery as a part of holistic, structural, and sustainable solutions to global health crises
