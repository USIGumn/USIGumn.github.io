Jack Hedberg — President

Majors: Biochemistry and Physiology

Minors: Microbiology and Pharmacology

Interests: I’m drawn to surgery because of the calculated, collaborative environment of the operating room, the diverse repertoire of skills used by surgeons, and the potential to change someone's life by personally addressing the disease or injury that has brought them into the world of the sick. I'm particularly attracted to trauma surgery because of its intense environment and because I want to help people through the most vulnerable points in their lives.

 Marissa Peyer — Director of Finance

Major: Biochemistry

Minor: Spanish

Interests: In general, I am really interested in working with people over a span of time. I really like the idea of getting to know my patients and, even though I might only be performing one surgery on them, to really get to know their story and to see them recover and return back to their normal life. Although I like the field of surgery in general, I really like orthopedic and trauma surgery. I think that each field holds its own kind of challenge and helps people to get back to their lives and what they enjoy doing. I am currently leaving my mind open to all of the fields in medicine, as I will only really know what I am truly passionate about after I explore my options in medical school.

Polina Prokhoda — Public Relations Chair

Major: Genetics, Cell Biology, and Development

Minor: Psychology

Interests: I'm interested in Ophthalmology, because, as a person with bad vision, I understand the importance of seeing clearly in a person's day to day life. Besides bad vision, the eyes can experience several other problems, including cataracts and infectious diseases. When faced with these problems, people become miserable, the eyes are a very sensitive organ, and need to remain healthy in order for a person to function to their full potential. I would like to help people see better, and eliminate eye problems from their every day lives. Specifically talking about surgery, Lasik surgery is a minimally invasive procedure, which can improve a person's vision, as well as their confidence and comfort. The surgery itself is almost artistic in nature, working in a very confined area that requires a keen and detail-oriented eye, and this type of work appeals to me.

Raghav Vadhul — Events Coordinator

Major: Biology

Minor: Neuroscience

Interests: I am interested in surgery because of its hands-on and evidence based natures. Although evidence based care is used in practically every field of medicine, it is especially important and impactful in the field of surgery. As new findings and cutting-edge technology are released regularly, surgeries are becoming more efficient and more complex and require a strong dedication to lifelong learning. This, combined with the hands-on practice of surgery, are what draw me to the field. Although I have this strong interest in surgery, I am actually undecided on which field I would like to go into. I have genuinely found every field of medicine fascinating, and I do not think I will be able to pick one until I (hopefully) explore them all more closely in medical school. This is also a good representation of what USIG itself stands for. Not only is the group for people who are certain they would like to go into surgery, but also for people like me who are still exploring the vast field of medicine.

Ajinkya Limkar — Director of Member Development

Major: Microbiology

Minor: Chemistry

Interests: I'm interested in surgery, specifically pediatric or trauma surgery, because it incorporates the the two things I value greatly; continuous acquisition of knowledge i.e lifelong learning, and helping others in order to make a positive impact on their lives. In regards to both surgical fields, especially trauma, patients are most probably at their lowest before having surgery, they're afraid, anxious, and distraught. The idea that someone can help them, bring them down to the level of calm just by doing their job, amazes me. The field of surgery also fascinates me because it incorporates the basic sciences of biology, chemistry, etc. with cutting-edge technological advancements. It would be the perfect mix of human contact, science and discovery, and helping others, it would really be my ideal career.
